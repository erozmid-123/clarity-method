The Gap Nobody Talks About

Your training prepared you for the child in the room.
Nobody prepared you for the parent in the waiting room.

"You know your interventions. You know the research. You've read the manuals. But then a parent sends a 9-paragraph email at 10pm questioning your entire treatment plan."

Or they cancel three sessions in a row because they "just don't see the progress." Or they tell you their child is fine — right when you're finally ready to start the exposure hierarchy.

Suddenly, all that clinical training feels irrelevant.

In child and family therapy, the parent is the intervention. They implement the homework. They regulate the home environment. They decide whether their child stays in treatment — or doesn't. And they're the ones writing the check.

If you can't bring a parent fully into the therapeutic process — if you can't earn their trust, manage their anxiety, and make them an ally instead of an obstacle — your clinical skills alone won't be enough.

This isn't a failure of technique. It's a gap in training. And it's one that most graduate programs never fill.

The Curriculum

12 Weeks. 20 Clinicians. One Transformative Cohort.

Live 90-minute sessions each week. Real case consultation. Clinical skills you'll use on Monday morning.

Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–3)

Week 01

The Clarity Framework

Why integrating EBPs is more effective than following one manual — and how to do it with clinical rigor. Your philosophy, your style, your method.

Week 02

Assessment That Drives Treatment

Functional assessment for children and families. Collaborative goal-setting with parents. What standardized tools miss — and what to do about it.

Week 03

The Parent Alliance Problem

The #1 reason child therapy fails. The paying-client dynamic, parent transference, and why the most clinically skilled trainees often lose parents fastest.

—

Phase 1 Complete

Foundation established. Moving into clinical integration.

Phase 2 — Clinical Integration (Weeks 4–8)

Week 04

Mastering the Parent Session

Psychoeducation that actually lands. Scripts for handling resistance. How to get parents to implement what you're teaching — consistently.

Week 05

CBT Precision Across Development

Cognitive interventions adapted for ages 5–18. Making the work playful without losing fidelity. The cognitive model explained in ways kids actually understand.

Week 06

DBT Skills for Youth

When DBT is the better fit. Distress tolerance and emotion regulation adapted for younger clients. Teaching skills to kids and parents simultaneously.

Week 07

The Integration Decision

How to decide — in the room — which approach to use and when to blend. Live case consultation. You will leave this week thinking differently.

Week 08

Behavioral Interventions That Stick

Behavior plans, exposure hierarchies, contingency management — translated into parent-friendly language. How to close the gap between what you recommend and what families actually do.

—

Phase 2 Complete

Integration mastered. Moving into advanced application.

Phase 3 — Advanced Application (Weeks 9–12)

Week 09

The Hard Cases

Anxiety, OCD, trauma, defiance — the presentations that stump most clinicians. Dr. Rozmid's approach to each. What the manuals skip. Real cases, real complexity.

Week 10

Building a Specialty Practice

How the RCC credential positions you for referrals and higher fees. How to talk about your specialty in a way that families immediately understand and value.

Week 11

Ethics & Family Systems

Confidentiality with minors. HIPAA across family members. Navigating split families, custody disputes, and complex family dynamics. Documentation that protects your decisions.

Week 12

Capstone + Certification

You present a case. You receive live feedback from Dr. Rozmid and your cohort. You earn your RCC credential. You leave as a Rozmid Clarity Certified™.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to already be working with kids and families? ↓

You should have some child or family cases currently, or be actively building that caseload. This is an applied program — you'll be bringing real cases to live consultation every week.

I'm pre-licensed. Can I apply? ↓

Yes. Pre-licensed clinicians working under supervision are welcome to apply. The RCC credential is awarded upon program completion regardless of licensure status.

Is the program recorded if I miss a session? ↓

Sessions are recorded for enrolled cohort members only. We strongly encourage live attendance — the consultation and community dynamics are where most of the transformation happens, and those don't translate to a recording.

Will this help me attract private pay clients? ↓

Yes — this is explicitly addressed in Week 10. Specialty credentials shift how families perceive your value. RCC clinicians are positioned to charge more and attract families willing to pay out of pocket for a specialist. Many graduates raise their rates within weeks of completing the program.

How is this different from a regular CEU training? ↓

CEUs give you information. This program changes how you practice. Small cohort, live consultation, case presentation, earned credential. There is genuinely no comparison.

When is the next cohort, and how many spots are left? ↓

Cohort [#] opens [DATE] and applications close [DATE]. Cohorts are capped at 20 clinicians and waitlists form quickly. We recommend applying early — your spot is not reserved until you've been accepted and enrolled.

What if I'm accepted but not ready for this cohort? ↓

Your application stays active and we'll notify you when the next cohort opens. You'll have priority consideration for the following cohort without reapplying.
